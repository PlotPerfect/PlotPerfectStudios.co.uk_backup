Behind the Build: Developing the CRE8.London Website from Vision to Functionality

By Plot Perfect Studios | July 7, 2025

At Plot Perfect Studios Ltd, we take pride in crafting custom websites that reflect a brand's identity while providing users with a seamless and engaging online experience. One of our recent highlights is the development of www.cre8.london — a cutting-edge website built for a signage supply company with bold ambitions and a growing customer base.

This blog takes you behind the scenes of the development journey — from early design discussions and UI/UX strategies, to launching a visual product catalogue, and our roadmap for integrating a full e-commerce experience in the near future.

Understanding the CRE8 Vision

From the outset, the founders of CRE8 Sign Supplies had a clear objective: create a sleek, user-friendly, and professional website that showcases their product range visually, provides easy access to key product information, and builds credibility with both new and existing clients. They wanted a website that stood out, not just for aesthetics but also for usability.

Rather than immediately launching with full e-commerce functionality, the CRE8 team opted to build out a strong foundation first — a visual product catalogue that could serve as both an online showroom and a point of contact for inquiries.

Phase One: Building the Visual Shop

Our initial development focused on crafting an interactive, visual-first experience. We designed a structure that allowed users to easily browse through product categories such as Cre8bond panels, adhesives and accessories, LED lightboxes, and more — all without requiring a login or shopping basket at this stage.

Key features in this initial build included:

  • Hover-based previews of product images to make browsing feel dynamic and intuitive.
  • Expandable product panels, allowing users to view product details, subcategories, and specifications in a clean layout.
  • QR code integration for WhatsApp and direct phone inquiries, enabling customers to quickly get in touch without filling out forms.
  • Fixed background visuals, stylish hover effects, and responsive design for seamless mobile compatibility.
  • Custom cursor, scrollable content panels, and smooth animations — all contributing to a modern, engaging user experience.

While this version doesn't include basket or checkout functionality, the current system serves as an ideal step one: showcasing the product offering and building online visibility while preparing for the next phase of expansion.

Design Approach and Technologies Used

To bring the vision to life, we employed a custom HTML, CSS, and JavaScript stack, avoiding templated platforms like Shopify or WordPress in favour of full control over the UI and performance. Every element of the site — from section layout to product interaction — has been hand-coded to ensure precision and future scalability.

The website structure was designed using a modular panel system, allowing different product categories and subcategories to be toggled in and out based on user interaction. This makes the site light on load and easy to maintain while keeping the experience highly visual.

For hosting and speed, we used GitHub Pages with custom domain routing via Fasthosts DNS. This provided a fast, secure, and scalable deployment pipeline while keeping overheads low — a win-win for a growing business.

Accessibility and SEO Considerations

From the ground up, the site was built with SEO in mind. Clean semantic HTML structure, optimized meta descriptions, and keyword-rich content blocks were implemented to give the site a solid start on Google’s indexing. Accessibility features were also considered — ensuring proper contrast ratios, alternative text for images, and mobile responsiveness across all devices.

The CRE8.London site was submitted to search engines and business directories to begin building organic traffic, with the visual shop acting as a powerful portfolio of what the company offers.

What Comes Next: Full Online Shop Integration

While the initial version focused on visual presentation and direct contact options, the next development milestone is already planned: full e-commerce functionality.

This future upgrade will introduce:

  • User login and registration system with email/password authentication.
  • Shopping basket and order tracking.
  • Product filtering and search to help customers quickly locate what they need.
  • Payment gateway integration, allowing secure checkout via credit/debit card or invoice.
  • Admin backend panel, enabling the CRE8 team to manage inventory, view orders, and update product details easily.
  • Customer dashboard, where users can see past orders, manage account info, and download invoices.

To prepare for this, the site has already been structured with scalability in mind. Our next steps will likely include integrating Firebase or a similar backend system for database and authentication functionality — keeping the site lightning-fast while adding robust features.

Building with the Brand in Mind

What makes CRE8.London stand out isn't just its functionality — it’s the attention to branding. The black and neon green colour scheme was carried through every element, from buttons to hover states, giving the site a distinct look that aligns with CRE8’s product identity. Even the hover animations and gallery displays were styled to feel high-tech and professional, mirroring the company’s innovation in signage.

We also integrated future-ready panels for Terms & Conditions, Privacy Policy, and even a Gallery Overlay that can be expanded into a full carousel viewer. These forward-thinking components ensure that as CRE8 grows, the site can scale with them — both functionally and aesthetically.

Final Thoughts

Developing CRE8.London has been a collaborative journey. It’s more than just a website — it’s a scalable digital platform that balances visual appeal with strategic business planning. By first establishing a strong visual presence online and preparing the architecture for e-commerce in the background, we’ve helped CRE8 launch confidently while laying the groundwork for long-term digital growth.

Whether you're browsing products today or planning to order directly in the near future, CRE8.London is built to evolve with you.

Custom Website Development vs WordPress Templates: Which Is Right for Your Business?

By Plot Perfect Studios | June 25, 2025

When launching or redesigning a website, one of the biggest decisions you’ll face is whether to go custom or use a pre-built WordPress template. Both options have their pros and cons, and your choice can impact everything from performance and scalability to user experience and long-term costs.

At Plot Perfect Studios Ltd, we specialise in custom website development — but that doesn’t mean WordPress templates don’t have their place. Let’s break down the differences to help you make the right decision for your business.

🧩 What Is a WordPress Template?

A WordPress template (or theme) is a pre-designed layout you can install and customize through the WordPress dashboard. Think of it as a “plug-and-play” solution — ideal for beginners or those needing a fast, affordable launch.

Common Features:
  • Drag-and-drop builders
  • Predefined layouts
  • Plugin integration
  • Basic customization (fonts, colors, images)

🛠️ What Is Custom Website Development?

Custom development means building your website from scratch, tailored entirely to your brand, users, and functionality needs. It’s a bespoke solution — no templates, no limitations.

Common Features:
  • Unique design built around your brand
  • Hand-coded performance optimisation
  • Custom features and integrations
  • Greater control over SEO and UX
  • Scalable architecture

🆚 Pros & Cons: Custom Development vs WordPress Templates

Feature WordPress Template Custom Development
Cost (Initial)✅ Lower upfront cost❌ Higher investment (but higher ROI)
Time to Launch✅ Fast setup❌ Longer development cycle
Design Flexibility❌ Limited to template structure✅ 100% tailored to your brand
Performance & Speed❌ Often bloated with unused code/plugins✅ Optimised for performance
Scalability❌ Can struggle with growing demands✅ Built to scale as your business grows
Security❌ Depends on theme/plugin integrity✅ Controlled security architecture
Ongoing Maintenance❌ Frequent updates & plugin conflicts✅ Clean, controlled code with minimal upkeep
SEO Performance❌ Relies on SEO plugins✅ Custom-coded for speed & crawlability

💸 ROI: Which Gives You More Value Over Time?

While a WordPress template might seem cost-effective at first, it can limit your growth and cost more in the long run due to:

  • Plugin conflicts
  • Performance issues
  • Rebuilds needed as you scale

Custom websites have a higher upfront cost, but they’re a long-term investment:

  • Optimised for conversions
  • Easier to maintain
  • Built around your goals and audience

🔧 Maintenance: Templates vs Custom

WordPress templates require frequent plugin and theme updates, and compatibility issues are common. You might find yourself spending hours troubleshooting — or worse, hiring someone to fix it.

Custom sites, especially those developed by Plot Perfect Studios, use clean, lightweight code with minimal third-party dependencies, meaning:

  • Fewer breakages
  • Easier to update
  • Less technical debt

We also offer monthly maintenance plans, so your custom site stays secure, fast, and up to date — without the stress.

🤔 Which Should You Choose?

Choose WordPress Templates if you…
  • Need a simple brochure-style site
  • Have a tight budget and short timeline
  • Don’t expect complex functionality or integrations
  • Are comfortable handling your own updates
Choose Custom Development if you…
  • Want a unique, branded experience
  • Have specific functionality requirements
  • Need scalability and long-term reliability
  • Want a professionally maintained website that grows with you

✨ Our Verdict

Templates are great for quick, budget-friendly projects — but if your business is serious about growth, branding, and performance, custom development is the smarter long-term play.

10 Key Steps to Building a Great Small Business Website

By Plot Perfect Studios | July 1, 2025

In today’s digital-first world, having a well-designed, functional, and optimized website isn’t just a luxury—it’s essential. Whether you're a local shop, a startup, or an established brand, your online presence plays a critical role in attracting and converting customers. Here are the 10 key steps to building a great small business website that not only looks professional but also delivers results.

1. Define Your Website’s Purpose

Before you start building, be clear about your goals. Are you looking to generate leads, sell products, showcase your portfolio, or provide information about your services?

Your website’s purpose should align with your business objectives. A service-based business might need contact forms and service pages, while an eCommerce site needs a shopping cart and secure checkout. Understanding your website’s core purpose guides every other decision in the design and development process.

2. Choose the Right Domain Name

Your domain name is your digital address—it should be simple, memorable, and reflective of your brand.

  • Keep it short and easy to spell.
  • Avoid hyphens or numbers if possible.
  • Use keywords if they make sense (e.g., “SmithPlumbingLondon.com”).
  • Stick to .com or .co.uk for trustworthiness and SEO benefits.
  • Use domain registrars like GoDaddy, Namecheap, or Fasthosts to secure your name.

3. Select Reliable Web Hosting

Web hosting determines how fast and reliable your website performs. For small businesses, shared hosting plans are budget-friendly and sufficient to start with, but as you grow, consider upgrading to VPS or cloud hosting.

  • 99.9% uptime
  • Free SSL certificates
  • Good customer support
  • Easy scalability
  • Providers like SiteGround, Bluehost, Fasthosts, or Hostinger are popular choices for small businesses.

4. Design with Your Audience in Mind

Your website should reflect your brand, appeal to your ideal customers, and guide them to take action. A clean layout, clear navigation, and mobile responsiveness are non-negotiables.

  • Stick to a consistent color scheme and typography.
  • Use high-quality visuals (avoid stocky or irrelevant images).
  • Highlight your USP (Unique Selling Proposition) on the homepage.
  • Keep the layout intuitive—make important information easy to find.
  • For custom branding, consider hiring a designer or using design tools like Canva Pro for consistency.

5. Create Valuable, Well-Structured Content

Content is what connects you to your visitors. It should be clear, engaging, and optimized for both users and search engines.

  • Homepage – Overview of your business and value proposition
  • About Us – Your story, team, and mission
  • Services/Products – What you offer, with detailed descriptions
  • Contact – Form, email, phone number, map, and social links
  • Testimonials – Build trust with real feedback
  • FAQ or Blog – Address common queries or share insights

Use SEO-friendly headings (H1, H2), bullet points, and internal links to help users and search engines navigate your site.

6. Optimize for Mobile Devices

Over 60% of web traffic now comes from mobile. Your website must look and function great on smartphones and tablets.

  • Mobile-responsive design (test with Google’s Mobile-Friendly Tool)
  • Clickable buttons are big enough for thumbs
  • Fonts are legible on small screens
  • Pages load quickly on mobile networks

Failing to optimize for mobile can hurt your SEO and user experience.

7. Implement On-Page SEO Best Practices

Search Engine Optimization helps your site rank higher on Google and reach your target audience.

  • Use relevant keywords in titles, headings, and content
  • Write unique meta titles and descriptions
  • Optimize images with ALT tags
  • Use descriptive URLs (e.g., /services/web-design not /page1)
  • Submit a sitemap to Google Search Console

Regular blog updates with valuable, keyword-rich content will also improve your rankings.

8. Integrate Analytics and Tracking Tools

Without data, you’re flying blind. Tools like Google Analytics and Search Console help you understand how people find and use your site.

  • Track traffic sources
  • See what pages perform best
  • Monitor user behavior
  • Identify and fix technical issues
  • Measure conversions and goals

This insight helps you make smarter decisions and continuously improve your website’s performance.

9. Set Up Security Features

Website security is vital, especially if you collect user data or run transactions.

  • Install an SSL certificate (HTTPS)
  • Use secure, updated plugins and software
  • Choose strong admin passwords and 2FA
  • Regularly back up your website
  • Consider installing a firewall or using services like Cloudflare

A secure website builds trust with users and protects your reputation.

10. Test, Launch & Maintain Your Website

Before going live, test every page, button, form, and link. Check your site across different browsers (Chrome, Safari, Edge) and devices.

  • Proofread all content
  • Test contact forms and payment gateways
  • Check for broken links
  • Ensure fast loading times

Once launched, treat your site like a living asset—update content regularly, monitor for errors, and keep software up to date.

Final Thoughts

A small business website is one of the most powerful marketing tools you can invest in. By following these 10 steps, you can create a professional, high-performing site that attracts customers, builds trust, and helps your business grow online.
